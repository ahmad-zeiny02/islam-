Islam is a Religion of Peace and Mercy

Islam is a religion of peace and mercy, a religion that promotes morality, justice, and tolerance among people. It is a religion that recognizes all the prophets who preceded the Prophet Muhammad and calls for belief in the one and only God, the great Creator who has no partner. Islam is not just a set of worship but a complete way of life that calls for equality among human beings, regardless of their color, gender, or social background.

In Islam, human values such as truthfulness, honesty, mercy, compassion, and benevolence are essential in daily dealings. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: ‘I was sent to perfect morals’. This means that Islam came to complete and reinforce the virtues that enlighten human life and make it a tool for good in society.

The five pillars of Islam - Shahada, Prayer, Zakat, Fasting, and Hajj - reflect the principles of spiritual and physical discipline and develop a sense of responsibility towards God and humanity. Prayer brings a Muslim closer to God five times a day, Zakat contributes to the fair distribution of wealth between the rich and the poor, fasting teaches patience and control of appetites, and Hajj symbolizes Islamic unity and brotherhood among people.

Islam is a religion that embraces science and knowledge and encourages people to think and reflect on the universe, as the first verse revealed to the Prophet Muhammad was ‘Iqra’, which is proof that science and knowledge are the foundation of Islam.

As a comprehensive religion, Islam endeavors to achieve balance in the life of the individual and society. It calls for justice in everything - in judgment, in dealing with others, and even in the relationship with oneself. Islam teaches us to be tolerant and to treat others with kindness even if they differ from us in religion or belief.
